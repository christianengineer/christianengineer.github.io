How to Solve Difficult Problems and Embrace the Unknown

Are you currently grappling with a challenging problem? The path to solving intricate issues involves answering key questions and engaging in tangible actions. Here's a method to guide you:

Step 1: Define the Problem and Impact

Answer the following questions to gain insight into the problem:

  1. What does the solution look like?
  2. Where, what, or whom does this solution impact?
  3. What can you learn from the journey while arriving at this solution?

Upon reflecting on the third question, you'll recognize the actual benefits you'll gain by working towards a solution. Suddenly, the thought of giving up will recede.

Step 2: Identify the Next Physical Action

Let's begin:

  • Pick one of the insights you listed for question 3.
  • Now answer this question: What is the next physical action I can take to make the smallest amount of progress?

To Learn JavaScript

Follow these physical actions:

  • With your hand, move your mouse cursor to your preferred browser icon.
  • Click to open a new browser window.
  • Position your fingers on the keyboard.
  • Type "How to print hello world in JavaScript" into the search field.
  • Press 'Enter' to initiate the search.
  • Navigate to a relevant tutorial or guide.
  • Read the instructions on printing "Hello, World!" in JavaScript, following any code examples provided.

To Learn DevOps

Follow these physical actions:

  • With your hand, move your mouse cursor to your preferred browser icon.
  • Press your mouse button to click and open a new browser window.
  • Place your fingers on the keyboard.
  • Type "DevOps Categories" into the search field of the browser.
  • Press the 'Enter' key with your finger to initiate the search.

Step 3: Continue Making Progress

After completing the first physical action, ask yourself a slightly modified question:

  • What is the next physical action that I can take to keep making progress?
