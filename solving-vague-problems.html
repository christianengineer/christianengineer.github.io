How I solve vague problems and embrace the unknown

  1. Break up the problem into smaller problems.
  2. Identify which small problem I can solve first, then solve it.
  3. Go onto the next small problem, and repeat.

I take these types of problems as opportunities to learn something new.

As I continued to solve more vague and ambiguous problems, the more confident, and less fear I felt in solving challenging problems.

Repetition is key.
